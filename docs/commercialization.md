# Commercialization Notes

Decision Lab is strong as a local-first decision operating product. It is not yet a complete hosted SaaS business.

## Best Wedge

Start with founder, operator, investor, and executive workflows where decisions are expensive, recurring, and reviewable.

Positioning:

- "Decision records that force evidence, opposition, and review."
- "A local-first operating loop for investment, finance, and management judgment."
- "Not another notes app; a decision governance system."

## First Paid ICP

Best early users:

- Solo founders making pricing, hiring, runway, and fundraising decisions
- Small investment teams tracking theses and change-my-mind conditions
- Fractional CFO/COO operators who need structured memos and follow-up reviews

Bad early users:

- Large enterprises needing SSO, procurement, DLP, and SOC 2 on day one
- Teams that only want generic chat or note taking

## Product Tiers

- Free/Open Source: local CLI, schemas, reports, backups
- Pro Local: polished desktop/web UI, encrypted local workspaces, packaged installer
- Team SaaS: accounts, organizations, RBAC, comments, approvals, shared review calendar
- Enterprise: SSO, audit exports, retention policies, admin controls

## Company-Grade Missing Pieces

- Hosted deployment
- Persistent database
- Account and organization model
- Role-based access control
- Billing and plan enforcement
- Collaboration primitives: comments, approval states, assignments
- Support runbook and operational monitoring

## 30-Day Validation Plan

1. Recruit 5 design partners who make real recurring investment, finance, or management decisions.
2. Have each partner log 10 real decisions with `decide`, `capture`, and `today`.
3. Measure whether they return weekly without being reminded.
4. Charge for the workflow before building a full SaaS shell.
5. Build hosted features only around repeated partner pain.
